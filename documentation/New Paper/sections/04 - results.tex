\section{Results and Discussion}
\label{sec:results}

We evaluate our proposed architectures on a \textbf{Sepsis-3 Strict Cohort} derived from MIMIC-IV v3.1, comprising 7,966 unique ICU stays. This cohort represents patients with life-threatening organ dysfunction (SOFA $\ge$ 2) and suspected infection, providing a rigorous clinical benchmark.

The experimental results demonstrate the progression from vanilla architectures to deep-gated injection (DGI v2). To validate that performance gains originate from infused medical semantics, we include a \textbf{Dummy-Control} (S-Dummy) where medical embeddings are replaced by random noise.

\subsection{Summary of Findings}
The results consistently indicate that: (i) SAITS backbones outperform Transformers and Diffusion models in high-missingness discriminative clinical data; (ii) Mask-Aware gating provides superior fallback logic; and (iii) the medical content of the embeddings is the primary driver of performance across all architectural families.
